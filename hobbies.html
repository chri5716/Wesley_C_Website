Hobbies & Activities

I enjoy activities that build focus, patience, and creativity. Below are three key hobbies with stories and why they matter to me.

Going Outside

I regularly walk trails and spend time outside to recharge. Fresh air improves my concentration and helps me approach schoolwork with renewed energy.

Personal story: On a summer walk I sketched a timeline for a class project that later became my presentation plan — being outdoors helped me think creatively.

Why it matters: The ability to step back and reflect improves my planning and problem-solving in team projects and assignments.

Playing & Listening to Music

I both play and listen to music. Playing requires focus and practice; listening helps me maintain sustained concentration during study blocks.

Personal story: I performed at a small community event and gained confidence speaking and performing in front of others.

Why it matters: Regular practice builds discipline — a quality I apply to study routines and professional development.

Fishing

Fishing is a relaxing hobby that also teaches planning and patience. It’s a chance to reflect and learn about adapting to changing conditions.

Personal story: During a rainy weekend trip I adjusted my approach and still caught fish — the experience reinforced adaptability and persistence.

Why it matters: Patience and adaptability are valuable in collaborative work, especially when faced with shifting project requirements.
